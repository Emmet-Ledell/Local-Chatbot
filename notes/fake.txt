Name: Dalinar Kholin
Aliases: Blackthorn (past reputation)
Allegiance: Alethkar, House Kholin
Rank/Titles: Highprince of War, Uncle to King Elhokar
Family: Brother—Gavilar (deceased); Sons—Adolin, Renarin
Theater: Shattered Plains (Alethi vs Parshendi)
Gear: Ancient Shardplate and Shardblade
Guiding Code: The Codes (discipline, sobriety, proper armor & conduct)

Role & Arc (Book 1)

Transition from ruthless Blackthorn to principled commander.

Enforces the Codes, alienating indulgent highprinces.

Receives highstorm visions of ancient times; repeated command: “Unite them.”

Struggles with doubt—his own and others’—about the visions’ authenticity.

Prioritizes soldiers’ lives over gemheart glory runs; seeks Alethi unity.

Notable Events (Book 1)

Highstorm visions depicting Knights Radiant, Desolations, and the abandonment of Shardblades.

Publicly adopts the Codes, curbing feasts, wine, and lax armor etiquette in the warcamps.

Political friction and tactical entanglement with Highprince Sadeas (bridge assaults, bait strategies).

Chooses honor in critical command decisions, even at personal/political cost.

Relationships & Conflicts

Elhokar (King): Loyal support mixed with concern about competence and paranoia.

Sadeas: From uneasy ally to open antagonist; conflict centers on Bridge tactics and trust.

Adolin: Devoted son who questions father’s visions and strictness; loyalty tested.

Renarin: Quiet, observant; relationship marked by protection and understanding.

Navani: Rekindled warmth and history; cautious in Book 1.

Themes

Honor vs. expedience — lives over loot, principles over politics.

Leadership & restraint — discipline as strength, not weakness.

Unity vs. factionalism — vision of a reunited Alethkar.

Truth under scrutiny — visions as guidance vs. madness.

Quick Reference (RAG-friendly)

Setting keywords: Shattered Plains, plateau runs, bridge assaults, gemhearts.

Vision keywords: Unite them, Knights Radiant, Desolations, abandoned Shardblades.

Policy keywords: The Codes, sobriety, armor discipline, orderly camps.

Conflict keywords: Sadeas betrayal, Bridge Four ethics, Elhokar paranoia.
